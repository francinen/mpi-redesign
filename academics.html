Academics

A primary goal of the program is to connect media theory learned in the classroom to real world experiences. Students are encouraged to put what they learn to use. Many of the MPI courses feature a service learning component or practicum that gives students the opportunity to volunteer in a community organization.

  • Compulsory In-Class Courses
  • Experiential Learning
  • Electives

  • MIT 2152: Media and Social Movements

    This course examines the relationships between media, social movements, and social change. By exploring struggles over issues such as democracy, the environment, human rights, gender equality, class relations, and race and ethnic relations, we will map the ways that participants use media, and the obstacles they confront.

  • MIT 3901: Activism and Mainstream Media

    Activists often consider corporate or state owned media unfriendly terrain. But such media can provide a crucial way to ‘get the message out.’ Using the ideas of experienced media activists, and examples from the practice of today’s social movements, this course investigates how to win the battle for public opinion in mainstream media.

  • MIT 3902: Alternative Media

    This course examines the definition, history, theory and practice of “alternative” media, and its differences from and relationship to “mainstream” corporate and state supported media. These issues are explored across a variety of media to critically assess the possibilities and limits of alternative media in enriching cultural diversity and democratic practice.

  • MPI Practicum

    This mandatory course combines work in the field of public interest media, participation in a special seminar and theoretically informed paper or project based on the practicum experience. Students will be partnered with a local organization, where they will gain practical experience in nonprofit/public interest communications.

    Possible practicum placements include Art for AIDS International, Unity Project, the Sentinel Project for Genocide Prevention, and Make a Wish Foundation.

  • Internship Opportunities

    In addition to the practicum, MPI students have the option of completing accredited internships with public interest organizations in Canada and abroad.

    Read about some of the organizations recent MPI students have worked with.

    • Canadian Journalists for Free Expression

      CJFE monitors, defends and reports on free expression and access to information in Canada and abroad. The Toronto-based NGO boldly champions the free expression rights of all people, and encourages and supports individuals and groups in the protection of their own and others' free expression rights.

    • Southeast Asian Press Alliance (SEAPA)

      SEAPA campaigns for genuine press freedom in Southeast Asia. Based in Bangkok, Thailand, it aims to unite independent journalists’ and press-related organizations in the region into a force for advocacy and mutual protection.

    • Centre for Independent Journalism

      The Centre for Independent Journalism (CIJ) in Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia works to develop a society that is democratic, just and free. CIJ strives to promote and defend the exercise of freedom of expression; ensure good policies and advocating legislative change; raise public awareness on freedom of expression/information; and facilitate marginalised voices through community media.
